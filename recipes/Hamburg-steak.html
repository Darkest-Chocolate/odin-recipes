Hamburg Steak

Japanese Hamburg Steak (ハンバーグ Hambāgu

Japanese hamburg steak is a juicy patty loaded with flavor and glazed with complimenting sauces. An all time favorite of the japanese household, think salisbury steak but better!!!

Ingredients

    For patty
  • 200 grams onion (finely diced)
  • 700 grams ground beef
  • 140 grams soft tofu
  • 50 grams panko (~1 cup)
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons vegetable oil
    • For Sauce
  • ½ cup dry red wine
  • ⅓ cup ketchup
  • 3 tablespoon chunou sauce (or a 50:50 mix of Worcestershire:ketchup)
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce

Steps

    Put the chopped onions and garlic into a microwave-safe bowl and cover with a lid or plastic wrap. Microwave the onions at 800 watts for 4 minutes. They should end up soft and be just starting to brown. Leave the onions covered and allow them to cool down enough to handle.Onions and garlic, which have been steamed in the microwave before adding to the Hamburg Steak mixture.
  1. When the onions have cooled a bit, add the beef, tofu, panko, egg, oyster sauce, nutmeg, and black pepper. Put some food-safe gloves on and knead the mixture together until it is uniform in color and texture.Meat mixture in a bowl for making Japanese Hamburg Steak
  2. Make eight oval patties about 1-inch thick, by tossing the meat back and forth between two cupped hands. This removes any air pockets that will cause your Hamburg Steak to fall apart when you cook it. If you're making the patties ahead of time, or plan to freeze them, be sure to place the patties on a layer of parchment paper. Otherwise, you can form them and put them straight into the pan Shaping the best Hamburg Steak Patties.
  3. Add the vegetable oil to a large pan with a lid and heat over medium heat until hot. You'll get more flavor in your sauce if you use an uncoated pan, however, it will make it harder to flip without breaking them, chose your pan carefully.
  4. Add the patties to the pan without overcrowding it. I usually do this in 2 batches of 4. Fry them on use side until they've formed a dark brown crust.Frying Japanese-style Hamburg Steak (Hambagu) in a pan.When they've browned on one side, use a spatula to flip them over carefully and brown the other side. The patties should be well browned on both sides, but they don't need to be cooked through all the way since we're going to steam them. Transfer them to a plate.Browned Japanese single serving meatloaf in a pan.
  5. Once you've fried all the patties, drain off any excess oil with a paper towel, being careful to leave the caramelized beef juices on the bottom of the pan. Put all eight patties back into the pan and then add the wine and cover with a lid. Steam these for 2-3 minutes.Steaming Hambagu in a pan with red wine.
  6. Remove the lid and transfer your hambagu back onto a plate. Add the ketchup, oyster sauce, and chunou sauce and stir briskly to emulsify the sauce.The sweet and savory sauce for Hamburg Steak.
  7. Once the sauce is nice and thick, add the patties back in one at a time, rolling each one around to glaze it with the sauce.
